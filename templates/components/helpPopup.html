MathJax explanation

You can type scientific and mathematical expressions using MathJax formatting! Surround your expressions in \( TEXT HERE \), \[ TEXT HERE \], or $$ TEXT HERE $$ to format them!

COMMON SYMBOLS (add this anywhere inside the \(...\)):

Output Code Description
\( \pm \) \pm plus minus symbol
\( \times \) \times multiplication symbol
\( \div \) \div division symbol
\( x^{a} \) x^{a} superscript/powering (replace x and a)
\( x_{a} \) x_{a} subscript (replace x and a)
\( \sqrt{x} \) \sqrt{x} square root (replace x)
\( \sqrt[n]{x} \) \sqrt[n]{x} nth root (replace n and x)
\( \le \) \le less or equal to
\( \ge \) \ge greater or equal to
\( \neq \) \neq not equal to
\( \Rightarrow \)
or
\( \rightarrow \)		 \Rightarrow
or
\rightarrow		 right arrow for chemistry
\( \text{Normal Text} \) \text{Normal Text} Normal text within math mode
\( \cancel{2} \) \cancel{2} Canceling terms
\( \boxed{x=5} \) \boxed{ x=5 } boxed expression
\( \frac{A+B}{C-D} \) {A+B \over C-D}
or
\frac{A+B}{C-D}		 fraction of A+B/C-D
\( \sum\limits _{i=1} ^{n} i^2 \) \sum\limits _{i=1} ^{n} i^2 sigma sum notation
N/A \limits puts the sub and super scripts directly above/below the operator(eg, \sum\limits_{i=1}^n)
\( \Delta \) and \( \delta \) \Delta \delta Greek letters (lowercase first letter for lowercase, ONLY SUPPORTS SPECIAL UPPERCASE LETTERS)
N/A \! connects two characters together. eg,\( abc \) becomes \( a \! b \! c \)
N/A \, \: \; \quad \qquad adds small spacing(increasing order). eg, \( 1 \, 2 \: 3 \; 4 \quad 5 \qquad 6 \)
